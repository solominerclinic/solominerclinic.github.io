Hydro Cooling Set Improperly

Hidden Hydro Cooling Killer — Why Your ASIC Dies Even With “Good” Temps

Congratulations to everyone following the hydro setup discussion.

I finally took pictures to show the problem many of us have without even realizing it.

For a long time I kept seeing ASIC footprints on the copper plate. I saw it so many times that I started questioning the universe for answers. Eventually, I found an explanation in a YouTube video, and now I want to explain the issue and solution to you guys.

Here is what happens:

As you can see in the pictures, the back plate has standoffs with nuts, and the pump screws have built-in stoppers. When you tighten the screws, the standoffs and stoppers meet each other first. The spacing between the PCB and pump still looks “correct,” so most of us never notice a problem.
But here is the catch.

This setup is supposed to work like a spring-loaded heatsink. In reality, the springs are barely applying pressure to the ASICs because the standoffs stop the screws too early.
When the pump runs, vibration causes the copper cold plate to slightly move and rub against the ASIC surface. Over time, this leads directly to overheating, damage, and eventually catastrophic ASIC failure.
The solution is simple:

Use M3 nuts with washers on the back, or add washers so the standoffs sit flush with the PCB surface. This allows the springs to finally create real mounting pressure on the ASICs.
Also:
• Tighten the screws in a diagonal pattern
• Do not overtighten
• Check that the springs are actually compressing and applying pressure to the ASICs
This small fix can save your miner and improve thermal contact significantly.
